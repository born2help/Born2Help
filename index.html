Born to Help

B2H (Burn to Help | Born for Humanity) is a humanitarian-first blockchain project designed to create real-world impact, not speculation. Transparency, patience, and long-term responsibility guide our work.

About Us

Our Story
B2H was created to explore a new way for technology to serve humanity. Unlike speculative digital assets, B2H connects participation with real-world humanitarian impact.

Our Purpose
- Embed compassion into blockchain technology
- Support verified humanitarian initiatives and aid for the poor
- Maintain transparency, accountability, and long-term trust

How We Work
- Fixed Supply Token: 500 billion B2H tokens, no future minting
- Burn-to-Help Mechanism: Each burn contributes to charity and reduces supply
- Separate Funds: Charity, operational, and treasury funds managed transparently
- Founder Stewardship: Guided ethically, not for personal gain

Our Values
Integrity over speed
Trust over hype
Patience over pressure
Humanity over profit

Who We Serve
Individuals, charities, NGOs, and communities in need who benefit from direct, responsible support

Our Commitment
Participation is voluntary, focus is long-term impact, not short-term speculation.

Transparency Framework

Transparency in B2H exists to protect trust — not to expose the vulnerable or invite harm.

What We Commit to Disclose

  • Mission purpose and ethical constraints
  • Governance structure and safeguards
  • Participation principles and risk warnings
  • High-level humanitarian impact summaries
  • Use-of-funds categories (non-exploitable)

What We Will Not Publicly Disclose

  • Exact wallet addresses or internal accounts
  • Detailed logistics of aid distribution
  • Personal identities of beneficiaries
  • Security-sensitive operational data
  • Private continuity designations

Withholding information protects people — not power.

Financial Communication Boundaries

No price targets, profit projections, or speculative forecasts. Financial transparency exists only within ethical boundaries.

Privacy as Protection

Public exposure of suffering leads to stigma and exploitation. B2H protects dignity by refusing to turn suffering into content.

Independent Accountability

Trusted ethical reviewers may privately verify practices. No reviewer claims control or influence.

Amanah (Trust)

Trust is sacred. Careless disclosure can cause harm. Responsible restraint is always preferred over harmful exposure.
